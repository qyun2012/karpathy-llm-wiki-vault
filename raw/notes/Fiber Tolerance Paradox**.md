### **Inflammation-Dependent Fiber Tolerance Model**

**Core Mechanism:** Fiber tolerance is determined by the 3-way interaction of **mucosal inflammation intensity × microbiota fermentation capacity × intestinal barrier integrity**—not by fiber itself.

**Clinical Decision Matrix:**

- **Deep remission** (FC <100): Safe rapid escalation → 20-25 g/d target
- **Clinical remission** (FC 100-250): Gradual escalation → 12-18 g/d
- **Mildly active** (FC 251-500): Minimal fiber (<8g) only after 6-8 weeks biologic stabilization
- **Moderately-severely active** (FC >500): **Absolute fiber prohibition**

**Critical Rules:**

- ❌ Maximum 2-3 g/week increment (>4 g/week = 74% symptom worsening; Zhang 2023 meta-analysis)
- ✅ Symptom priority over theory (if stable at 10 g/d with FC=200, maintain 10 g/d—don't force to 20)
- ✅ Adequate hydration (2.5 L/d) essential; dehydration worsens bloating

### **4-Dimensional Symptom Monitoring + Red Flag Protocol**

De-escalation triggers (stop immediately if ANY triggered):

1. Bloating score ≥3 consecutive days >5/10
2. Stool frequency increase ≥2/day sustained >5 days
3. New abdominal pain >3/10 sustained >2 days
4. Blood/mucus in stool
5. Calprotectin increase >50 μg/g from baseline

### **Special Population Adjustments**

- **Pediatric IBD**: Half escalation speed (1-2 g/week); target = age + 5 (g/d)
- **CD + obstruction history**: FC must be <100; no flares; soluble fiber only; half escalation speed
- **UC patients**: Same protocol as CD; prioritize oats/barley; avoid legumes until FC <100
- **IBS-D comorbidity**: Double escalation time (1 g/week); psychological intervention; low-FODMAP compatible fiber

**Resolution of the Paradox:**

High-fiber protection in observational cohorts (Nurses' Health, PREDIMED) comes from **healthy or remission-phase patients**; clinical intolerance in practice comes from **active inflammation patients**.

This isn't fiber's contradiction—it's **hidden stratification by inflammation status**:

- Healthy + high fiber = ✅ Protective (robust microbiota, tolerates SCFA)
- Remission + high fiber = ✅ Protective + sustaining (restored microbiota)
- **Active inflammation + high fiber = ❌ Harmful** (dysbiosis → rapid fermentation bloating; barrier breakdown → translocation)

**Therefore: Fiber is not universally contraindicated, but requires inflammation-status-dependent precision dosing.**
