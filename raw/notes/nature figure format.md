## Resolution & File Formats

- **Line art** (graphs, schematics): Submit as **vector** (PDF, EPS, AI, SVG) whenever possible. If raster, use **1,000+ DPI**
    
    .
    
- **Photos / micrographs**: **300 DPI** minimum
    
    .
    
- **Composite figures**: **600 DPI** minimum
    
    .
    
- **Preferred formats**: `.ai`, `.eps`, `.pdf`, `.svg` for vector art; layered `.psd` or `.tif` for editable layered art; `.tif`, `.png`, `.jpg` for bitmaps
    
    .
    

## Typography

- **Font**: **Arial** or **Helvetica** (sans-serif only). Use Symbol font for Greek letters
    
    .
    
- **Text size**: **5–7 pt** at final print size (optimum is ~7 pt)
    
    .
    
- **Panel labels**: **8 pt bold lowercase** (`a`, `b`, `c`) placed in the **upper-left** corner of each panel
    
    .
    
- **Line weight**: Minimum **0.25 pt**
    
    .
    

## Color & Accessibility

- **Color mode**: **RGB** for original research content
    
    .
    
- **Avoid red–green** combinations for contrast (colorblind-unfriendly). Use green–magenta, blue–orange, or viridis palettes instead
    
    .
    
- Do not rely on color alone; add shapes, patterns, or direct labels
    
    .
    
- Avoid rainbow/jet color scales
    
    .
    

## Display Item Limits

- **Articles**: Maximum **6 display items** (figures + tables combined)
    
    .
    
- **Extended Data**: Up to 10 Extended Data figures/tables allowed (online only, peer-reviewed, not counted in the 6-item limit)
    
    .
    
- **Legends**: Maximum **≤350 words** each. Begin with a brief bold title sentence, then describe what is depicted—not the experimental results or methods
    
    .
    

## Submission Workflow

- **Initial submission**: Figures can be embedded in the manuscript PDF/Word file. High-resolution files are **not required** at this stage, but images must be clear enough for referees
    
    .
    
- **After acceptance**: You will be asked to submit production-quality figures following the guidelines above
    
    .
    

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Rasterized line art** — always export graphs as vector (PDF/EPS) rather than PNG/JPEG.
    
2. **Unreadable text** — check your figure at actual print size (89 mm or 183 mm wide on screen); if you struggle to read it, reviewers will too.
    
3. **Wrong figure width** — do not submit figures at random dimensions; use exactly 89 mm or 183 mm.
    
4. **Missing or inconsistent panel labels** — use bold lowercase `a`, `b`, `c` in the upper-left.
    
5. **Red–green color schemes** — this is a frequent rejection trigger.
