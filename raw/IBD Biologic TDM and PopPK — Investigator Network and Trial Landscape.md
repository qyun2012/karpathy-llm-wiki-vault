## Trial 1: MOVE-IT (NCT06788340) ★ Highest Priority

Full title: MOdel-Informed Precision Dosing (MIPD) of Ustekinumab and Vedolizumab in IBD  
Sponsor: Odense University Hospital, Denmark  
Status: Not yet recruiting | Start: Sept 2025 | Completion: Aug 2027  
Phase: 4 | n=166 | 6 Danish centers | ClinicalTrials.gov: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06788340](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06788340)

### Design

Open-label RCT. Intervention: PK model + PK-PD targets used every 4 weeks to decide dose/interval for UST or VDZ. Control: physician judgment (blinded to drug levels until study end). Blood + stool samples every 4 weeks. Endoscopy at 48 weeks.

### Eligibility

- UC or CD, stable on VDZ or UST ≥3 months
    
- Calprotectin ≤200 µg/g + PRO2 within remission range at entry
    
- Age ≥18; excludes indeterminate colitis, stoma, fistulizing disease as primary indication
    

### Primary Endpoint

Steroid-free remission at 48 weeks (PRO2 ≤4 for CD / PRO2=0 for UC, AND calprotectin ≤200 µg/g)

### Secondary Endpoints

Mucosal healing (SES-CD <3 / UCEIS ≤1), cost-effectiveness (QALYs via EQ-5D-5L), drug persistence, fatigue (VAS-F), WPAI, inflammatory burden (CRP/albumin/FCP every 4 weeks), disease flares (steroid use or surgery)

### Sub-protocols

- n=20 Odense patients: endoscopy + colonic biopsies at baseline AND completion (biobank)
    
- n=20 patients: intestinal ultrasound at baseline and completion
    

### Protocol Paper

Frimor C, Steenholdt C, ..., Burisch J, ..., Ainsworth MA.  
BMJ Open Gastroenterology 2025. DOI: [10.1136/bmjgast-2025-001985](https://doi.org/10.1136/bmjgast-2025-001985)

### Strategic Notes for Your Work

- MOVE-IT enrolls stable maintenance patients (calprotectin ≤200 at entry) — your cohort covers the full disease activity spectrum including active flares
    
- MOVE-IT is a Nordic RCT with homogeneous population — your cohort provides Asian real-world data with heterogeneous disease severity and prior biologic exposure
    
- Framing: "MOVE-IT tests MIPD under controlled conditions; our real-world Chinese cohort provides the external validity that an RCT cannot"
    
- Timeline overlap: MOVE-IT completes Aug 2027 — your UST PopPK paper should be published before then to establish priority

## Trial 2: Asan IFX TDM for Perianal CD (NCT06051253)

Full title: TDM-based Infliximab Treatment for Active Perianal Fistulizing Crohn's Disease  
Sponsor: Asan Medical Center, Seoul, South Korea  
Status: RECRUITING | Start: Nov 2023 | Completion: June 2026 (primary); June 2027 (full)  
Phase: 4 | n=86 | Single center | ClinicalTrials.gov: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06051253](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06051253)

### Design

RCT. TDM arm: target trough level ≥10 µg/mL; escalate 5→10 mg/kg if TL <10 (from week 14 onward). Standard arm: same schedule, TDM measured but not used for decisions.

### Eligibility

- Perianal fistulizing CD, bio-naïve, ≥1 draining fistula
    
- Failed ≥2 conventional treatments
    
- Korean ethnicity only (explicit exclusion criterion)
    
- Age 19–80
    

### Primary Endpoints

1. Clinical remission at both week 50 AND week 54 (no draining fistula on compression, without seton)
    
2. MAGNIFI-CD score change from baseline at week 54
    

### Secondary Endpoints

Clinical response (≥50% reduction in draining fistulas), MAGNIFI-CD score = 0, CRP <0.6 mg/dL, infliximab trough levels (median at weeks 22/30/38/46/54), IBDQ, adverse events

### Key Limitation

Korean ethnicity only — limits direct comparison but confirms demographic relevance for Asian IBD populations.

### Collaboration Opportunity

Joint analysis potential: Asan's TDM-optimized perianal fistula cohort + your 746-case IFX cohort (luminal CD focus) = complementary phenotype coverage with shared Asian demographic context.

## Trial 3: PROMPT-IBD (NCT06758024)

Full title: Proactive and Early Infliximab Monitoring and Optimization in IBD  
Sponsor: Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile  
Status: RECRUITING | Start: Jan 2025 | Primary completion: Jan 2026  
Phase: NA | n=72 | 5 Chilean centers | ClinicalTrials.gov: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06758024](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06758024)

### Design

RCT 1:1. Intervention: iDOSE (adaptive Bayesian dashboard) guides dosing from week 2 of induction (TL measured at infusions 2 and 3). Target: TL 7–20 µg/mL at week 14. Control: Standard dosing based on clinical parameters only. Population: hospitalized moderate-to-severe IBD patients failing IV corticosteroids.

### Primary Endpoint

Proportion achieving TL 7–20 µg/mL at week 14

### Secondary Endpoints

CS-free clinical remission at week 52, sustained CS-free remission, CRP normalization, FCP normalization, primary non-response (week 14), secondary loss of response (weeks 14–52), ADA-free survival, surgery, hospitalization

### Relevance to Your Work

Focus on hospitalized acute patients overlaps with your ASUC/UC surgery prediction cohort. Uses iDOSE (Bayesian) — same methodological framework as your MAP estimation approach. Results expected early 2026.

## Trial 4: TDM-Guided Adalimumab Optimization (NCT03261102)

Full title: Therapeutic Drug Monitoring Guided Early Optimization of Adalimumab in Crohn's Disease  
Sponsor: Waqqas Afif, McGill University Health Centre  
Status: ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING | Start: Jan 2017 | Completion: June 2025  
Phase: NA | n=200 | 6 Canadian centers | ClinicalTrials.gov: [https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03261102](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03261102)

### Centers

Calgary, Vancouver, London (ON), Ottawa, Montreal (MUHC), Sherbrooke

### Primary Endpoint

Remission at week 12: HBI <5 AND (CRP <5 mg/L OR FCP <250 µg/g)

### Secondary Endpoints

Clinical response, TDM levels + ADA at weeks 8/12, steroid-free rate, SIBDQ, complications (hospitalization, surgery, adverse events)

### Note

Results expected 2025. Watch for publication — this will be the largest RCT of TDM-guided adalimumab optimization in CD to date.

## Critical Paper to Read Now (Overlaps Your UC Surgery Prediction Work)

Niyigena E, Hoffert Y, Afif W, ..., Dreesen E.  
"Personalized infliximab rescue therapy to maximize colectomy-free survival in patients with acute severe ulcerative colitis."  
J Crohn's Colitis 2026 Mar 10; 20(3).  
DOI: [10.1093/ecco-jcc/jjag029](https://doi.org/10.1093/ecco-jcc/jjag029)

### What They Did

Multicenter retrospective popPK + parametric time-to-event model (n=72 ASUC, 7 centers). Strongest predictor of 90-day colectomy: clearance-normalized AUC between weeks 2–4 (AUCw2-4/CL ratio), AUROC 0.79. Patients with log(AUCw2-4/CL) <5.79 classified as high-risk (sensitivity 83%, specificity 85%). Built into an interactive dosing tool.

### Co-authors of Note

Papamichael K (Harvard/BIDMC), Kobayashi T (Kitasato, Tokyo), Vermeire S + Ferrante M + Verstockt B (KU Leuven), Vande Casteele N (UCSD), Battat R (U Montreal), Afif W (McGill)

### Your Competitive Position

||Niyigena et al. 2026|Your UC cohort|
|---|---|---|
|n|72 ASUC|437 (26 surgery events)|
|Population|Western multicenter|Chinese single-center (FAH)|
|Method|popPK + time-to-event|Fine-Gray competing risk|
|Predictor focus|AUC/CL (PK-based)|Clinical + biomarker predictors|
|Output|Interactive dosing tool|Risk stratification score / nomogram|

  

Your advantages: Larger n, Asian population (unrepresented in their dataset), competing risk framework explicitly handling non-surgery events, and ASUC risk stratification tool targeting clinical decision-making at admission.
