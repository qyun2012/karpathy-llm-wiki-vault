mitarget 5042 is a DFG-funded interdisciplinary research group of experts within the field of chronic inflammatory bowel diseases. In the coordinated program this economically important and prototypically complex disease is specifically studied.

As patterns of gut microbiome dysbiosis in patients with inflammatory bowel diseases are inconsistent among published studies more microbiome studies in early (incident) patients that are treatment-naïve and in high-risk individuals are needed.

The main aims in miTarget are:

![mitarget_Abb1](https://www.uksh.de/uksh_media/Dateien_uksh_Forschung/Bilder/mitarget_Abb1-height-188-width-865.png "mitarget_Abb1")

**Project Z:** Metabolomics, microbial community modelling and data integration

**Project INF:** Data management, bioinformatics and statistics

**Project 1:** Early microbiome changes and its antigenic potential in individuals at high-risk for inflammatory bowel diseases

**Project 3:** Pregnancy as a perturbation principle of the intestinal microbiota: impact on intestinal inflammation and inflammation-associated cancer

**Project 4:** The role of tobacco smoke-induced changes of the microbiome in shaping risk for IBD

**Project 5:** Microbial modulation of glycolysis and its impact on inflammation and cancer

**Project 7:** Microbiome therapeutics as an innovative concept in IBD

**Project 8:** Cultivation and characterization of IBD-relevant microbes

**Project 10:** Bacterial evolution during chronic inflammation and its potential for ancestral state restorative therapy
