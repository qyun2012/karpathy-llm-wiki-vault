Exclusive enteral nutrition (EEN) is a first-line induction therapy in children with active Crohn’s disease. Now, a new trial (CD-HOPE; NCT02201693) published in *The Lancet Gastroenterology & Hepatology* has investigated nutritional therapy in the maintenance of drug-free remission in paediatric patients. Across 21 hospitals in France, 100 patients with Crohn’s disease (aged 6–18 years) in clinical remission after EEN induction therapy were randomly assigned (1:1) to receive either cyclic EEN (for a 2-week period every 8 weeks, at least 6 cycles) or daily partial enteral nutrition for 52 weeks.

After 1 year, 49% (24 of 49) of patients in the cyclic EEN group had relapsed compared with 76% (39 of 51) of patients in the partial enteral nutrition group (adjusted OR 0.29 (95% CI 0.13–0.70); *P* = 0.0051). A total of 19 adverse events were identified, and 6 were possibly related to study treatment.
