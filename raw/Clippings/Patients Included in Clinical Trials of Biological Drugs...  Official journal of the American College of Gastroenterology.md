Plain Language SummaryThis study examined the real-world applicability of clinical trial criteria for biologic treatments in inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) patients at a Spanish hospital. Out of 477 patients starting biologics, only 16.9% met the criteria of pivotal randomized clinical trials, and only 8% met the criteria of newer trials. Patients with ulcerative colitis were less likely to qualify than those with Crohn's disease (23% vs 4%). Despite this, both eligible and noneligible patients showed similar outcomes in terms of response, remission, adverse events, and treatment persistence. These findings suggest that while many IBD patients don't meet trial criteria, biologics can still be effective and safe, highlighting the need for clinical judgment in treatment decisions.
