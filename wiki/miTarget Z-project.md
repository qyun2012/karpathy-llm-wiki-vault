The **miTarget Z-project** is ==a central collaborative platform within the **miTarget** research consortium==, a DFG-funded research unit ([FOR 5042](https://gepris.dfg.de/gepris/projekt/426660215?language=en)) focused on the microbiome as a therapeutic target in **inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD)**. [[1](https://www.mitarget.org/research/z/), [2](https://www.uksh.de/Wissenschaft/Forschung/Forschungsgruppen/miTarget+_The+Microbiome+as+a+Therapeutic+Target+in+Inflammatory+Bowel+Diseases.html)]

**Core Objectives**

The Z-project integrates **metabolomics** and **metabolic modelling** to bridge the gap between basic research and clinical applications. Its primary goals include: [[1](https://www.mitarget.org/research/z/)]

- **Investigating Metabolism**: Studying the role of metabolism in microbiome-host interactions during IBD.
- **Identifying Shifts**: Using metabolomics to find disease-associated changes in metabolites within the microbiome and the host.
- **Mechanistic Modeling**: Elucidating how these interactions contribute to disease through computational modelling.
- **Data Integration**: Acting as a hub for microbial community modelling and data integration across the entire consortium. [[1](https://www.mitarget.org/research/), [2](https://www.mitarget.org/research/z/)]

**Leadership and Research Groups**

The project is led by two main expert groups:

- **[Heinzmann Group](https://www.mitarget.org/people/dr-rer-nat-silke-heinzmann/)** (Munich): Focused on **metabolomics** to profile metabolites in various biosamples.
- **[Kaleta Group](https://www.mitarget.org/people/prof-dr-rer-nat-christoph-kaleta/)** (Kiel): Led by Prof. Dr. Christoph Kaleta, specializing in **Medical Systems Biology** and the metabolic modelling of host-microbiota interactions. [[1](https://www.mitarget.org/research/z/), [2](https://www.mitarget.org/people/dr-rer-nat-silke-heinzmann/), [3](https://www.mitarget.org/people/prof-dr-rer-nat-christoph-kaleta/), [4](https://www.mitarget.org/people/dr-jan-taubenheim/)]

**Research Context**

Within the [miTarget consortium](https://www.mitarget.org/), the Z-project supports several other specialized research projects (P1–P10), such as those targeting intestinal yeasts, the impact of pregnancy on the microbiota, and tobacco smoke-induced microbiome changes.
