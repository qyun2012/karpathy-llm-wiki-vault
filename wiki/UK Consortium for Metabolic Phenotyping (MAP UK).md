The **UK Consortium for Metabolic Phenotyping (MAP UK)** is ==a strategic partnership of leading UK universities and research centres designed to standardise and enhance metabolic phenotyping (metabolomics) capabilities across the country==. [[1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36828894/), [2](https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=MR%2FS010483%2F1)]

Its primary mission is to create a harmonised platform for high-quality metabolic data, which is essential for discovering disease biomarkers and understanding how genes, diet, and environment interact to influence human health. [[1](https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=MC_PC_12025), [2](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12615335/), [3](https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=MR%2FS010483%2F1)]

**Core Objectives & Focus**

- **Standardisation:** Developing community-accepted, "fit-for-purpose" [frameworks for evaluating the quality and reliability](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41596-022-00801-8) of both targeted and untargeted metabolomics assays.
- **Capability Building:** Expanding UK research capacity through cross-site training, protocol exchange, and the development of novel analytical methods to fill gaps in molecular coverage.
- **Disease Research:** Applying metabolic phenotyping to priority areas such as **cardiovascular disease, cancer, mental health,** and **Alzheimer's disease** to improve early diagnosis and patient stratification.
- **Population Health:** Supporting large-scale epidemiological studies, including the [UK Biobank](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S258900422401037X), to inform future public health policies and screening programmes. [[1](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41596-022-00801-8), [2](https://figshare.le.ac.uk/articles/journal_contribution/A_proposed_framework_to_evaluate_the_quality_and_reliability_of_targeted_metabolomics_assays_from_the_UK_Consortium_on_Metabolic_Phenotyping_MAP_UK_/22229995), [3](https://gtr.ukri.org/projects?ref=MR%2FS010483%2F1), [4](https://heart.bmj.com/content/heartjnl/107/14/1123.full.pdf), [5](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7730184/), [6](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S258900422401037X)]

**Key Partner Institutions**

The consortium links several major phenotyping facilities and academic departments, including: [[1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36828894/), [2](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41596-022-00801-8)]

- **University of Birmingham:** Home to the [Phenome Centre Birmingham](https://research.birmingham.ac.uk/en/projects/uk-consortium-for-metabolic-phenotyping-map-uk/).
- **Imperial College London:** Including the [National Phenome Centre](https://www.imperial.ac.uk/clinical-phenotyping-centre/resources/).
- **University of Liverpool:** Centre for Metabolomics Research.
- **University of Leicester:** NIHR Leicester Biomedical Research Centre.
- **Other Partners:** Universities of Aberdeen, Cardiff, and Warwick. [[1](https://www.futurelearn.com/info/courses/metabolomics/0/steps/10696), [2](https://mscape.climb.ac.uk/), [3](https://www.nihr.ac.uk/about-us/what-we-do/working-with-partners/other-funders), [4](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36828894/), [5](https://www.leco.com/news/leco-announces-metabolomics-training-collaboration-in-the-uk/)]

**Key Resources & Outputs**

- **Quality Control (QC) Framework:** A tiered approach (discovery, screening, qualification, and validation) to ensure metabolomics data is robust and reproducible across different labs.
- **Methodology Databases:** Creation of untargeted method characterisation databases and informatics pipelines for comprehensive metabolome coverage.
- **Training & Outreach:** Providing educational resources for the research community and engaging the public through lab tours and advocates.
